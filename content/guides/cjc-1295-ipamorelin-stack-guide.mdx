# CJC-1295 + Ipamorelin Stack: The Complete Research Guide

The CJC-1295 and Ipamorelin combination is widely considered the gold standard for growth hormone (GH) optimization research. Unlike other GH peptide combinations that sacrifice selectivity for potency, this stack achieves both — producing a high-amplitude GH pulse without meaningfully elevating cortisol, prolactin, or other stress hormones.

This guide covers the science behind why this specific combination works, what the research shows, and how researchers approach protocol design.

## Understanding the Two-Peptide Mechanism

To understand why CJC-1295 + Ipamorelin works so well together, you need to understand the body's natural GH release mechanism.

**GH release depends on two signals:**
1. **GHRH (Growth Hormone-Releasing Hormone)** — produced in the hypothalamus, tells the pituitary "release GH now"
2. **GHSR (Ghrelin receptor) activation** — provides an amplification signal that increases the amplitude of the GH pulse

CJC-1295 mimics GHRH. Ipamorelin mimics ghrelin at the GH secretagogue receptor (GHSR). Together, they trigger both pathways simultaneously — the result is a GH pulse several times larger than either compound produces alone.

## CJC-1295 (Without DAC)

CJC-1295 without Drug Affinity Complex (DAC) is a 29-amino acid analog of endogenous GHRH. It binds GHRH receptors in the anterior pituitary with higher affinity than native GHRH due to modified amino acid substitutions at positions 2, 8, 15, and 27.

**Key pharmacokinetics:**
- Half-life: approximately 30 minutes
- Onset of GH release: within 15–30 minutes of subcutaneous administration
- Duration of GH elevation: 2–3 hours

**What it does:** CJC-1295 increases the amount (amplitude) of GH released per pulse. It does not significantly change pulse frequency. Research models show it can increase GH peak concentrations by 3–7x depending on dose.

## Ipamorelin

Ipamorelin is a cyclic pentapeptide and highly selective GH secretagogue. It was designed to trigger GH release through the GHSR-1a receptor without the off-target effects seen in earlier secretagogues (like GHRP-6's dramatic cortisol and prolactin elevation).

**Key pharmacokinetics:**
- Half-life: approximately 2 hours
- Selectivity: highly selective for GH release, minimal cortisol or prolactin elevation
- Mechanism: activates GHSR-1a receptor in pituitary somatotrophs

**What makes Ipamorelin unique:** Unlike GHRP-2 or GHRP-6, which elevate cortisol and prolactin significantly, Ipamorelin shows a very clean hormonal profile in research. This selectivity makes it the preferred GHSR agonist for research protocols where hormonal precision matters.

## Synergistic Effect: Why the Combination Works

When administered together, CJC-1295 and Ipamorelin act on two separate receptor systems that have a multiplicative (not merely additive) effect on GH release.

Research published in the Journal of Clinical Endocrinology & Metabolism and subsequent animal studies have shown:

- **Combined GH release is 2–10x greater** than either compound alone
- The pulse pattern remains physiological (pulsatile, mimicking natural GH secretion)
- No significant cortisol, prolactin, or ACTH elevation with Ipamorelin
- IGF-1 levels measurably increase within 2–4 weeks of consistent use

This is because GHRH receptor activation (CJC-1295) and GHSR activation (Ipamorelin) are synergistic at the level of intracellular signaling — both converge on cAMP and PKA pathways, amplifying each other's signal beyond simple addition.

## Research Protocol Overview

Standard research protocols for CJC-1295 + Ipamorelin administration:

**Dosing:**
- CJC-1295 (without DAC): 100–200 mcg per injection
- Ipamorelin: 200–300 mcg per injection
- Typical ratio: 1:1 to 1:1.5 (CJC:Ipamorelin)

**Frequency:**
- 2–3 injections per week
- Some protocols use 1x daily injection before sleep

**Timing:**
- Best administered on an empty stomach (3+ hours after eating)
- Pre-sleep administration aligns with the natural GH surge that occurs during deep sleep
- Avoid carbohydrates and fatty foods 30–60 minutes before and after administration (insulin blunts GH response)

**Duration:**
- 12–24 week research cycles are common
- Some researchers use 5-day on / 2-day off protocols to prevent desensitization

## What the Research Shows

Human trial data is more limited than animal studies, but several published works are relevant:

- **Teichman et al. (2006)** — CJC-1295 (with DAC) showed dose-dependent GH and IGF-1 increases that persisted for days in human subjects
- **Ionescu & Frohman (2006)** — Review of GHRH analogs shows combination with GHSR agonists produces synergistic GH release
- **Multiple animal models** — Combination protocols show improved body composition, reduced adiposity, and improved bone density markers over 8–24 week periods

## Storage and Handling

**Lyophilized (unreconstituted):**
- Store at 2–8°C (refrigerator)
- Stable for 12–24 months when refrigerated

**Reconstituted:**
- Use bacteriostatic water for reconstitution
- Store reconstituted peptide at 2–8°C
- Use within 28–30 days of reconstitution

**Reconstitution guide:**
- Standard: 2mg vial + 2mL bacteriostatic water = 1mg/mL (1000mcg/mL)
- 200mcg dose = 0.2mL drawn
