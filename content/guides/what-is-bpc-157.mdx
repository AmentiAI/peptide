# What is BPC-157?

**BPC-157** (Body Protection Compound-157) is a synthetic pentadecapeptide consisting of 15 amino acids. It is derived from a protective protein found in human gastric juice. Originally studied for its role in protecting the gastrointestinal tract, BPC-157 has since attracted significant research interest across a wide range of healing and regenerative applications.

## Mechanism of Action

BPC-157 exerts its effects through several interconnected pathways:

### 1. Growth Hormone Receptor Interaction
BPC-157 modulates the growth hormone/insulin-like growth factor 1 (GH/IGF-1) axis, which is critical for tissue repair and regeneration.

### 2. Angiogenesis Promotion
One of its most studied effects is the upregulation of VEGF (Vascular Endothelial Growth Factor), promoting the formation of new blood vessels — a critical step in wound healing.

### 3. Nitric Oxide Pathway
BPC-157 appears to interact with the nitric oxide system, which plays roles in vasodilation, inflammation regulation, and cellular signaling.

### 4. Tendon and Ligament Healing
Research in animal models shows BPC-157 significantly accelerates the healing of tendons and ligaments by increasing tendon cell survival and migration, and promoting expression of growth factor receptors.

Studies have demonstrated BPC-157's effects in the following areas:
