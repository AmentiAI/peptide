# BPC-157 Dosage Protocols for Research

This guide summarizes dosage parameters observed in BPC-157 research. All protocols described here are derived from preclinical research and should be adapted to specific research contexts.

## Standard Research Dosing Parameters

The majority of animal model research on BPC-157 has used doses in the range of **1–10 mcg/kg** body weight, which translates to approximately **250–500 mcg/day** for typical research subjects.

### Common Research Doses

| Application Area | Typical Dose Range | Frequency |
|-----------------|-------------------|-----------|
| General healing research | 250–500 mcg | Daily |
| GI research | 10 mcg/kg | Daily |
| Tendon/ligament | 250–500 mcg | Daily |
| Systemic administration | 250 mcg | Daily |

## Administration Routes

### Subcutaneous (SubQ) Injection
The most commonly used route in research. BPC-157 is injected into the subcutaneous fat layer, typically in the abdomen. This route provides:
- Predictable absorption
- Ease of administration
- Sustained release profile

### Intramuscular (IM) Injection
Used in research when faster absorption is desired or for studies targeting specific muscle groups. Slightly faster onset compared to SubQ.

### Oral Administration
Some research has explored oral BPC-157, taking advantage of its partial stability in gastric acid. Oral research doses are typically 5–10x higher than injectable doses due to reduced bioavailability. This route is most relevant for GI-focused research.

## Timing Considerations

### Half-Life Impact on Dosing
BPC-157's plasma half-life of approximately **4 hours** means that a single daily injection results in periods of lower circulating concentrations. Some research protocols split the dose into two administrations (AM/PM) to maintain more consistent levels throughout the day.

### Fasted vs Fed State
Unlike some peptides, BPC-157 does not appear to require fasting for optimal absorption. GI research often administers it in close proximity to meals.

## Stability of BPC-157

### Lyophilized Powder
- Store at -20°C for long-term storage
- Refrigerate (4°C) for shorter-term use
- Avoid freeze-thaw cycles

### Reconstituted Solution
- Use bacteriostatic water for multi-use vials
- Store reconstituted peptide at 4°C
- Use within 30 days
- Avoid exposure to light

## Quality Indicators for Research

When sourcing BPC-157 for research applications, critical quality metrics include:

1. **Purity** — HPLC purity of ≥99% is considered research grade
2. **Sequence confirmation** — Mass spectrometry verification
3. **Sterility testing** — Essential for injectable research applications
4. **Certificate of Analysis** — From an independent third-party laboratory
