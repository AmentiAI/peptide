# Ipamorelin: The Most Selective Growth Hormone Secretagogue

Ipamorelin occupies a unique position in growth hormone research. Developed as part of Novo Nordisk's search for selective GH releasing compounds in the late 1990s, Ipamorelin solved a fundamental problem with earlier GH secretagogues: specificity.

Previous compounds like GHRP-2 and GHRP-6 effectively stimulated GH release, but also triggered significant increases in cortisol, prolactin, and adrenocorticotropic hormone (ACTH) — complicating research results and introducing unwanted hormonal effects. Ipamorelin changed this by achieving selective GH stimulation through a highly targeted receptor interaction.

## Mechanism of Action

Ipamorelin is a cyclic pentapeptide that acts as an agonist at the Growth Hormone Secretagogue Receptor 1a (GHSR-1a), also known as the ghrelin receptor. This receptor is expressed primarily in:
- Pituitary somatotrophs (GH-producing cells)
- Hypothalamus
- Various peripheral tissues

When Ipamorelin binds GHSR-1a in pituitary somatotrophs, it triggers:
1. Activation of Gαq/11 proteins
2. Phospholipase C (PLC) activation
3. IP3-mediated calcium release from the endoplasmic reticulum
4. Calcium-dependent exocytosis of GH-containing granules

The result: a clean, selective GH pulse that does not meaningfully activate pathways leading to cortisol or prolactin release.

## Pharmacokinetics

Published pharmacokinetic data for Ipamorelin:

- **Half-life:** ~2 hours after subcutaneous administration
- **Bioavailability (SC):** Good — subcutaneous injection provides reliable absorption
- **Time to peak GH:** 15–30 minutes post-injection
- **Duration of GH elevation:** 2–3 hours
- **Receptor binding:** High affinity for GHSR-1a; low affinity for other receptors

The 2-hour half-life means more frequent dosing (2–3x daily) is required compared to GHRH analogs with longer half-lives. However, this also means more precise control over GH pulsatile release timing.

## Published Research Highlights

Several key studies have characterized Ipamorelin's effects:

**Raun et al. (1998) — Novo Nordisk:** The original characterization study showing Ipamorelin's high selectivity compared to GHRP-2 and GHRP-6. Demonstrated that Ipamorelin produced robust GH release without cortisol or prolactin elevation in rat models.

**Johansen et al. (1999):** Dose-response characterization in pigs showing dose-dependent GH stimulation with Ipamorelin. No adverse effects on cortisol or other stress hormones noted.

**Multiple body composition studies:** Rodent studies using Ipamorelin over 8–24 weeks show consistent improvements in lean mass, reduction in adipose tissue, and improved bone mineral density — consistent with GH-mediated metabolic effects.

**Sleep quality:** GH release normally peaks during slow-wave sleep. Research models show that administering Ipamorelin before sleep amplifies this natural peak, with downstream improvements in sleep architecture markers.

## The GHRH + GHSR Stack Principle

Ipamorelin is often combined with a GHRH analog (like CJC-1295 or Sermorelin) based on a well-established pharmacological principle:

**GHRH (CJC-1295 or Sermorelin) + GHSR agonist (Ipamorelin) = multiplicative GH release**

This is because GHRH and GHSR pathways are synergistic at the pituitary level:
- GHRH increases cAMP → increases GH pulse amplitude
- GHSR activation triggers intracellular calcium release → triggers GH secretion
- Both signals together produce a much larger GH pulse than either alone

Research shows the CJC-1295 + Ipamorelin combination can produce GH pulses 5–10x larger than Ipamorelin alone, while maintaining the selectivity profile of Ipamorelin.

## Research Protocol Design Considerations

When designing Ipamorelin research protocols, researchers typically consider:

**Single compound protocols:**
- 200–300 mcg subcutaneous, 2–3x daily
- Best administered on an empty stomach (reduces insulin interference with GH release)
- Pre-sleep dosing to amplify natural GH surge
- Duration: 8–24 weeks for body composition endpoints

**Stack protocols with CJC-1295:**
- Ipamorelin: 200–300 mcg per injection
- CJC-1295 (without DAC): 100–200 mcg per injection
- Frequency: 2–3x per week (co-administered)
- Some researchers use: daily Ipamorelin + 2x weekly CJC-1295

**Variables to control in research:**
- Nutritional status (GH response is blunted by insulin/glucose)
- Sleep quality (GH is strongly tied to sleep architecture)
- Stress levels (cortisol is antagonistic to GH signaling)
- Timing consistency

## Storage and Reconstitution

**Lyophilized:** Refrigerate at 2–8°C. Stable 12–18 months.

**Reconstituted:**
- Use bacteriostatic water
- Target concentration: 1000mcg/mL (2mg vial + 2mL BW)
- 200mcg dose = 0.2mL on a U-100 syringe
- Use within 28–30 days, keep refrigerated
